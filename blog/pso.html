Operational semantics of PSO storage system

A state consists of a memory \(M : \operatorname{Loc} \rightarrow \operatorname{Val}\) and a function (buffer) \(B : \operatorname{Tid} \rightarrow ( (\operatorname{Loc} \rightarrow \operatorname{Val}^{\ast})^{\ast}\)

Initially, \(M_0 = \lambda x. 0\), \(B_0 = \lambda i. (\lambda x. \varepsilon)\)

The propagate rule:

\(\displaystyle \frac{x \in \operatorname{Loc}, B (i) = s \cdot S, s (x) = b \cdot v}{M, B \xrightarrow{i : \varepsilon} M [x \mapsto v], B [i \mapsto (\lambda y.x = y ? b : s (y)) \cdot S]}\)

and:

\(\displaystyle \frac{B (i) = \varepsilon \cdot S}{M, B \xrightarrow{i : \varepsilon} M, B [i \rightarrow S]}\)

Store-Store fence rule:

\(\displaystyle \frac{l =\operatorname{SF}}{M, B \xrightarrow{i : l} M, \lambda i.B (i) \cdot \varepsilon}\)

Write rule:

\(\displaystyle \frac{l = W (x, v), B (i) = S \cdot s}{M, B \xrightarrow{i : l} B [i \rightarrow S \cdot (s [x \rightarrow v \cdot s (x)])]}\)

The remaining rules are the same as TSO.
